My Favourite Game Doesn't Exist

Except it does, kinda.

My actual favourite game is hard to pin down, and varies depending on when you ask me. It's somewhat nebulous between Blood on the Clocktower, Minecraft, or the topic of this page: 1,000 Blank White Cards.

In 1,000 Blank White Cards (1kBWC from here on), there is a shared deck of the eponymous Blank White Cards in the center of the table. Players draw a hand to start the game, and take turns playing a card from their hand to either themself, another player, or the middle of the table, before drawing a card to end their turn.

The game continues like this until a player tries to draw a card from an empty deck, when the game ends and the player with the most points is declared the winner.

"But the cards are blank?" I hear you ask.

For now.

There is one small little rule that makes 1kBWC so interesting. As well as the deck, there is a small pile of pens and markers in the center of the table. If a player has a Blank White Card in their hand at any time, they are free to draw and write on it at their leisure, and create rules and effects that occur when the card is played.

And the final rule: I lied about the deck. It doesn't contain just Blank White Cards. It contains the deck from last game, with a handful of Blank White Cards added to grow the deck between games.

At first, players create cards that give them points, remove points from other players, or draw extra cards. Over time, as they play with the medium further, cards appear that set fire to cards to remove them from the deck, or allow you to take a sharpie to an existing card to strategicly remove a word and change the effect. Cards appear that have players take an existing card from the deck and improve it *between games*.

It's this expansion of the boundaries of a game that interests me so much. While the game itself claims the player with the most points is the winner, players who create memorable cards or moments that remain from game-to-game are the real winners.

I don't remember how many times I have won. I remember how many "Gun" cards have been made. The first was flipped over in its sleeve, making it unusable, and the second was set on fire. The third and fourth were eaten, and the fifth has yet to be created.

My deck is an amalgam of cards created by my friends and myself that tell a story no other deck could, and I love it.
